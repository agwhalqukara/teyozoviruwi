    Who Is Andy Samberg?

    Andy Samberg is a comedian, actor, singer, and writer, who has written a number of shows including the NBC comedy series Saturday Night Live.

    Born on August 18, 1978, in Berkeley, California, Samberg’s interest in creative writing surfaced during his high school days.

    He then began taking writing classes before proceeding to the University of California, Santa Cruz and the New York University (NYU)’s Tisch School of the Arts.

    Years later, Samberg went on to write the NBC sketch comedy series Saturday Night Live, which aired from 2005 to 2012.

    As a singer, Samberg identifies with the comedy music group The Lonely Island.

    As an actor, Samberg has appeared in several films including Hot Rod, Space Chimps, I Love You, Man, the Cloudy with a Chance of Meatballs, That’s My Boy, Celeste and Jesse Forever.

    Other films he starred in are the Hotel Transylvania film series, Popstar: Never Stop Never Stopping, Storks, Palm Springs, America: The Motion Picture, Chip ‘n Dale: Rescue Rangers, and the NBC, police sitcom Brooklyn Nine-Nine.
