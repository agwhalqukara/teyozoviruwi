    Lily Safra, a billionaire socialite who is Brazilian-Monegasque, amassed substantial wealth from her four marriages.

    She possessed a substantial art collection in addition to owning the renowned Villa Leopolda on the French Riviera.

    Lily Safra was born Lily Watkins in Porto Alegre, Brazil, on December 30, 1934.

    She was the child of Annita Noudelman de Castro, an Uruguayan of Russian-Jewish descent, and Wolf White Watkins, a Czechoslovak railroad engineer who relocated to South America during the electrification of Brazilian railroads.

    She was raised in Rio de Janeiro before relocating to Montevideo, Uruguay, with her family.

    She gathered a sizable collection of works of art and was the proprietor of the renowned Villa Leopolda on the French Riviera.

    Safra was listed as having US$1.3 billion in wealth on Forbes’ 2018 list of the world’s billionaires and ranked 1756. Her estimated net worth in June 2022 was $1.3 billion.

    When she married banker Edmond Safra, she had a keen interest in philanthropy, and that enthusiasm has persisted through their union.
