    Lil Rel Howery Overview and Wiki

    Early Life and Education

    Lil Rel Howery, born Milton Howery Jr., embarked on his journey in life and comedy in Chicago, Illinois. Born on December 17, 1979, he grew up on the West Side of Chicago, a setting that would later influence much of his comedic style and content.

    He was born to Nancy and Milton, and his upbringing in this vibrant city laid the groundwork for his future career in entertainment.

    Howery’s educational journey began at Providence St. Mel School, where he attended from fifth grade to ninth grade. This period was crucial in shaping his early academic and social experiences.

    He then transferred to Crane High School, a move that would prove pivotal in his pursuit of comedy. It was at Crane High School where Howery’s talent for humor and storytelling began to surface prominently.

    Early Career and Television Breakthrough

    Howery’s foray into comedy saw him performing in Chicago, honing his skills and developing his unique style. His television debut came in January 2007 on the reality TV competition Last Comic Standing, marking his entry into the national comedy scene.

    The same year, he appeared on P. Diddy Presents: The Bad Boys of Comedy on HBO, further showcasing his comedic talent.

    In 2009, he participated in America’s National Night Out Against Crime in Chicago, demonstrating his commitment to community engagement alongside his entertainment career.

    Rise to Prominence

    In 2012, Howery’s career took a significant turn when he, along with five other comedians, starred in Fox’s revival of the 1990s sketch program In Living Color. Although the show was canceled in 2013, it opened more doors for Howery.

    He went on to work as a writer, producer, and regular cast member of the truTV sketch comedy series Friends of the People. His versatility and talent in both writing and performing comedy were becoming increasingly evident.

    The Carmichael Show and Stand-Up Success

    2015 marked a pivotal year for Howery as he began co-starring as Bobby Carmichael on the NBC sitcom The Carmichael Show alongside the show’s creator, Jerrod Carmichael. This role significantly boosted his profile in the television industry.

    In early 2016, Netflix added Howery’s first exclusive solo stand-up special, Kevin Hart Presents: Lil Rel: RELevent, for streaming, solidifying his status as a stand-up comedian.

    Acclaim and Recognition

    Howery’s career reached new heights with his performance in Jordan Peele’s satirical horror film Get Out (2017), where he played the memorable character Rod Williams. This role earned him the 2017 Best Comedic Performance MTV Movie & TV Award, a testament to his outstanding acting and comedic abilities.

    Rel and Beyond

    On May 10, 2018, Fox picked up Rel, a sitcom starring Howery in the lead role, which he also created and co-produced. The show, loosely based on his life as a divorced father, premiered on September 9, 2018.

    Although short-lived, Rel was a significant milestone in Howery’s career, showcasing his skills as a creator and producer.

    Diverse Roles and Continued Success

    Howery continued to expand his acting repertoire, starring alongside Ryan Reynolds in the comedy film Free Guy and playing the character Bishop on the HBO Max sitcom South Side from 2019 to 2022. His ability to adapt to various roles in different genres has been a hallmark of his career.

    Stand-Up Specials and Recent Projects

    In 2019, HBO released Howery’s second stand-up special, Lil Rel Howery: Live in Crenshaw. His third special, I said it. Y’all thinking it., was filmed in his hometown of Chicago and released in 2022.

    He also starred in several movies from 2022 to 2023, including I Love My Dad, Deep Water, and Vacation Friends 2, showcasing his versatility as an actor.

    Personal Life

    Off-screen, Howery’s life has had its share of ups and downs. His marriage to Verina Robinson ended in 2017, and he faced a legal incident in 2016. Despite these challenges, he has continued to thrive professionally.
